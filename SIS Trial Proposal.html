TRIAL BRIEF

The Proposal

6-week operating strategy trial · Studios 5–6 · Division 3 · Night shift

This is the six-week operating strategy trial. It validates three interconnected systems: the new tiered bonus structure, the 6-studio stagger scheduling model, and the continuous progression system with reliability tiers. Run on Division 3 (entry-level gamers) during the night shift. Measure cancel rate stability, SPS distribution, bonus predictability, and gamer satisfaction before rolling out to the full night pool.

01 · WHAT WE'RE TESTING

Three systems, validated simultaneously.

The six-week trial runs three initiatives in parallel. Each one is measured independently so we can isolate success signals and identify what works.

System 1 of 3

NBA Bonus Economics

Tiered win bonuses, division championship bonuses and reliability multipliers are now judged against a simple payback test: the new calculator moves the NBA shift from £3.51K to £4.51K, and breaks even at a 0.78 percentage-point reliability gain.

  • Tiers: Academy → Rookie → Regular → Specialist → Elite → Champion
  • Multipliers range from 0% (Academy) to +35% (Champion)
  • All tied to rolling 8-week SPS average
  • Reference: NEW NBA BONUS STRUCTURE&CALCULATOR.xlsx
System 2 of 3

Scheduling Model

6-studio stagger with 10-minute cadence, 32-minute match slots, and a fatigue‑safe 1v1 rotation (max 3 consecutive, all pairings present). Studios 4–6 truncated to 21 matches per night (vs. standard 22).

  • Night shift: 18:30–07:00 (11.5 hours paid, 1 hour unpaid break)
  • 10-min stagger ensures continuous bank activity
  • 21-match cap allows for natural rhythm breaks
  • Doubles rotation smooths fatigue distribution
System 3 of 3

Progression System

Continuous career tracking with 8-week rolling SPS average and automatic promotion/relegation. No seasonal resets. Career milestones unlock reliability tiers.

  • SPS formula: 0.45 density + 0.25 narrative + 0.15 reliability + 0.15 settlement
  • "Career in the Studio" tiers compound with bonus multiplier
  • Promotions begin Week 2 (sufficient rolling data)
  • Natural stratification expected by Week 5
02 · TRIAL SCOPE

Tight boundary. Maximum isolation.

The six-week trial is scoped to Division 3 (entry-level studios) during the night shift only. All 8 gamers start at Academy tier. This isolation lets us measure the systems against a homogeneous baseline and avoid confounding variables from mixed tiers or day-shift dynamics.

Studios 5 and 6 are the operational unit. Each night, we rotate 4 gamers per studio from the night pool. The stagger, match slots, and bonus structure are identical across both. Break and settlement protocols mirror the existing night shift.

04 · ON-CALL MODEL

Standby gamers and activation cost.

For the trial period, one standby gamer per shift. If not called in, they receive £50/day flat compensation. If called in, they receive full daily pay (base + wins + bonuses) plus an additional £50 on-call premium. Full 6‑studio operation maintains 1 standby/shift.

This creates a safety margin without penalizing standby gamers. At a 15% call-in probability (conservative industry baseline), expected on-call cost per shift is approximately £72 (1 standby × £50 × 0.85 flat + 1 standby × full daily × 0.15 call rate).

05 · PROGRESSION SYSTEM

Continuous tracking and career milestones.

Unlike seasonal resets, the progression system runs continuously. Performance accumulates in real time. The 8-week rolling SPS average is the primary metric for tier position.

SPS Formula
0.45 × event_density + 0.25 × narrative_delta + 0.15 × reliability + 0.15 × settlement_accuracy
Event density measures participation rate. Narrative delta tracks story contribution. Reliability captures cancellation avoidance. Settlement accuracy rewards clean event resolution.

★

Champion

Current division title holder (1 per division) AND 8+ wins per shift average. +35% multiplier. Highest earnings tier. Unlocks division championship bonus.

Trial expectations: All 8 gamers start at Academy. By end of Week 2 (14 shifts), those with strong event density and low cancellations should reach Rookie. By Week 5, we expect natural stratification with some holding Rookie, some possibly Regular if they've been promoted to a higher division.

06 · SUCCESS CRITERIA

What "pass" looks like after 6 weeks.

01

Cancel rate drops below baseline

S5–S6 cancel rate falls below 3% (target improvement from current ~5% baseline). The reliability multiplier should incentivize attendance.

02

SPS distribution shows tier separation

By Week 5, we expect meaningful SPS differentiation across the 8 gamers. Academy vs. Rookie vs. emerging Regular should be visually distinct in the distribution.

03

Bonus payout variance is predictable

Week-to-week bonus payouts show consistent patterns (no wild swings). Variance is explainable by win count and tier, not anomalies.

04

Gamer retention is 100%

All 8 gamers complete the full six-week trial. No mid-trial dropouts from dissatisfaction with pay, schedule, or progression mechanics.

05

On-call activations match assumption

Actual call-in rate stays within 10%–20% range. If wildly lower or higher, it signals scheduling or capacity issues.

If 4 out of 5 criteria pass, the trial is green-lit for expansion to full Division 3 night. If 3 or fewer pass, we adjust mechanics and run another six-week operating strategy trial. If all 5 pass, we proceed to full 6-studio Night rollout planning and prepare Day expansion as a separate capacity decision.
