THE QUESTION · NBA PILOT

If NBA runs across 6 Night studios and 4 Day, and most gamers work 1–4 shifts a week, what does the tournament actually look like?

Short answer: the binding NBA model is Night 6 studios + Day 4 studios (24/7), with the existing Night shift (Studios 5–6, Division 3) used as the 4-week live validation window inside the 6-week leadership pilot. Each studio runs 21–22 matches in 32‑min slots with a 4‑gamer cohort cycling through a fatigue‑safe rotation. The Night pool needs ~67 unique gamers, the Day pool ~46 (~113 NBA gamers total). The ladder still works — and the unit of competition becomes per‑shift performance scored against a rolling benchmark, with a real per‑studio peer set every gamer actually faces.

Scope · NBA pilot first

This deck is now NBA‑only. EAFC and eAMF inherit the structure later — once the 6‑studio shift model is operational, the cancel‑rate gain is measured, and the SPS weights are regressed on real shift data. Numbers below describe the NBA restructuring; do not generalise them across titles.

OBSERVATION 01

24 on, 43 off — every day, no exceptions.

Night: 6 studios × 4 gamers = 24 concurrent slots. The 67‑gamer Night pool delivers exactly that through a cadence mix: gamers work 1–4 shifts/wk standard (matching sample availability data) while rotating off‑days. Day runs 4 studios (16 concurrent, ~46‑gamer pool). No gaps, no over‑staffing.

OBSERVATION 02

Peer overlap is structural.

With 24 of 67 working any given day, the overlap rate is 36%. But committed gamers (4/wk) share most of their shifts and routinely face each other inside a studio's 4‑gamer cohort. The fatigue‑safe rotation guarantees every gamer faces every other within a shift. A real peer set exists.

OBSERVATION 03

Multiplier distribution is bottom-heavy — by design.

The pool spreads across 6 career multiplier tiers: 1 Champion (+35%), 4 Elite (+30%), 7 Specialist (+12.5%), 13 Regular (+7.5%), 19 Rookie (+2.5%), 23 Academy (0%). Only 1 gamer per shift holds Champion status. Standard max is 4 days/wk (2 exceptions at 5/wk for business needs). The pyramid shape ensures most cost sits at the base while top earners are genuinely elite.

OBSERVATION 04

SPS rolling average absorbs cadence variance.

Okafor (4/wk) gets 4 SPS evaluations this week. Vega (2/wk) gets 2. The 8‑shift rolling window means Vega's average updates every ~3 weeks while Okafor's refreshes in under 2 — cadence dictates volatility, not ceiling.

03 · THE FIX — RE‑DEFINE THE COMPETITION UNIT

Track the whole career. Rank on the last 8 weeks. No resets.

The system tracks a gamer's full career history — every shift, every SPS score, every tier position. But the metric that drives current ranking is the 8‑week rolling SPS average: long enough to smooth noise, short enough to reward real improvement. A gamer's full career record stays on file; their active tier position reflects recent form.

SHIFT PERFORMANCE SCORE · SPS
SPS = 0.45 × event_density_z // matches, goals/baskets, swings vs baseline
    + 0.25 × narrative_delta_z // lead changes, clutch moments, late drama
    + 0.15 × reliability // on‑time, zero voids, feed uptime
    + 0.15 × settlement_accuracy // zero disputes, clean market close

Final coefficients should be regressed on the 6-week leadership pilot. The working formula is stable enough for the 4-week live validation window: it rewards event density, keeps the broadcast narrative visible, and makes reliability a measurable pay signal rather than a soft expectation.

OUTPUT 01

One ranked pool, not isolated studios.

Every gamer has a career record and a current active tier. The studio cohort still matters, but rank is not trapped inside a single booth.

OUTPUT 02

Promotion has a visible basis.

Promote when the rolling SPS average crosses a threshold. Relegate when it drops below. The rule is auditable and explainable.

OUTPUT 03

Cadence variance is absorbed.

A 4-shift/week gamer refreshes the active sample quickly; a 1-shift/week gamer moves more slowly. Both are scored on the same structure.

OUTPUT 04

Pilot data determines the weights.

The six-week pilot produces the data needed to tune SPS weights before full Night expansion or future EAFC/eAMF inheritance.

ECONOMIC DECISION

Use the 6-week leadership pilot to validate the NBA Night model before expanding the cost base.

The economics support a contained test: Night-only restructure has a clear annualized ROI case, while the live validation window protects Day shift and non-NBA titles from premature change.
