SIS · INTERNAL DIAGNOSIS
05 · WHY THE CURRENT MODEL FAILS

The format produces the behaviour
it claims to prevent.

01

Burnout by schedule

A 4-minute stagger with no breaks, no variety, no stakes. Gamers play identical matches for eight hours. Cancellations are the pressure valve.
02

No progression

A good week and a bad week look the same. There's no "up" to play for, and no visible penalty for coasting.
03

Flat reward

Compensation tracks hours, not reliability or performance. Pays the same for a player who cancels and one who doesn't.
04

No stakes per match

Every match is interchangeable. A player has nothing to lose by skipping one — because nothing about the next one changes.

SIS · INTERNAL HOW A SHIFT RUNS
08 · LIFE OF A SHIFT — FIVE STEPS, ONE LOOP

A repeatable cycle that builds narrative,
not fatigue.

STEP 01
Seed
Players placed into tiers from prior shift's final standings.
STEP 02
Play
Round-robin inside each tier across the shift window. Every match counts toward the table.
STEP 03
Table
Live standings update after every match. W/L/pts visible on-air and internally.
STEP 04
Promote
Top of each tier ascends one level. Tier 1 winner earns the shift's Champion title.
STEP 05
Relegate
Bottom of each tier descends one level. Creates urgency from minute one.
↻ LOOP · EVERY SHIFT, EVERY DAY, EVERY WEEK
WEEKLY ROLLUP
14 shifts per week = 14 micro-championships. Top-of-table point totals crown a Player of the Week per title.
MONTHLY RESEED
Full ladder reseed based on a 4-week rolling average. Prevents entrenchment; lets rookies climb fast.
SEASONAL FINAL
Quarterly cross-title Super Shift — Tier 1 champions from each title play a showcase broadcast.
