Six studios, ten minutes apart. A new match closes every ~10 minutes.

Night shift begins. Studio 1 tips off at 19:00. Ten minutes later Studio 2. By 19:50 all six studios are running, each on its own 32‑minute slot cadence. A new NBA match closes roughly every 10 minutes on the bank — a continuous feed for broadcast, betting and the gamer leaderboard. Studios 4–6 lose their last match to avoid overlap with the incoming Day shift.

CROSS‑STUDIO VARIATION · FRESH FEEL
6 rare‑pair sets

Each studio's 4‑gamer cohort uses a different rotation grid where all 6 pairings appear but at different frequencies. Channel‑jumping between studios feels fresh because the marquee matchup is always different. Cross-division variation (D1 vs D2 vs D3) adds another layer of variety for punters.

03 · PER‑STUDIO ROTATION — FATIGUE‑SAFE SCHEDULING

4 gamers, 22 matches, 11 each — max 3 in a row, min 2 per block, everyone plays everyone.

Inside each studio, the 4‑gamer cohort cycles through 22 matches in a fatigue‑safe rotation. Each match is 1v1 (2 play, 2 rest); matches run 4×5‑min quarters with potential 3‑min OT (up to 4 times) in 32‑min slots. Each gamer plays exactly 11 of 22 matches (50%), with a hard cap of 3 consecutive games and no more than two blocks‑of‑3 per shift — all triples front‑loaded into the first half so the second half runs on blocks of 2 or fewer. All 6 pairings appear with near‑balanced counts (3–4 each), giving managers precise control over matchup quality.

FATIGUE‑SAFE ROTATION · WHY IT MATTERS
Max 3, first half only

Each match is 4×5‑min quarters with potential 3‑min OT (up to 4 times) in a 32‑min slot. Without limits, gamers could play 6+ in a row — over 2 hours of continuous competition. The hard cap of 3 consecutive games (no more than twice per shift) guarantees rest blocks of 2+ matches between intensity peaks. Critically, all triples are front‑loaded into the first half of the shift — late‑shift fatigue compounds with match length, so the second half runs entirely on blocks of 2 or 1.

EVERYONE PLAYS EVERYONE
All 6 pairings

The rotation ensures all 6 possible 1v1 pairings (AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD) appear across the shift. Pairing counts are near‑balanced (3–4 per pair) — and because the schedule is predictable, managers can assign specific gamers to specific slots. High‑skill matchups get the 4‑count pairings; weaker matchups get the 3‑count slots. This turns the schedule into a planning tool, not just a rotation — directly addressing today's biggest bottleneck in shift operations.

05 · REVENUE IMPACT — WHY THIS RECOVERS CANCELLATIONS

The 6‑studio stagger is a cancellation firewall at NBA scale.

Most SIS match cancellations trace back to the same handful of causes: a gamer no‑shows, a studio has a tech fault, a feed drops. In the current model these failures scale linearly — one problem cancels one match, or the whole studio‑shift. The 6‑studio fatigue‑safe structure with on‑call standby (1/shift; £50/day flat or regular pay + £50 if activated) turns most of those failures into recoverable incidents. Where the recovery actually comes from

The £3.77M (NBA 24/7) headline breaks down across five specific failure modes. Each is addressed by a specific property of the 6‑studio structure with on‑call standby.

01 · £1.28M / YR

Gamer no‑show / last‑minute schedule change

Today: A no‑show forces a last‑minute schedule change — usually 1 direct cancellation, but the remaining gamers drop to a 3‑gamer schedule. Morale drops, error rates climb, and further cancellations cascade. Sometimes multiple studios fall to 3‑gamer setups or a studio goes down entirely. Now: On‑call standby slots in before the shift's first match. Studio stays at 4 gamers; no morale hit, no cascade.

02 · £0.88M / YR

Late shift start — 15‑min rule

Today: If the studio comes up 15 minutes late, the event is cancelled outright. Now: The 10‑min stagger gives each studio more pre‑show preparation time for schedule changes, reducing the chance of hitting the 15‑minute threshold. The structured handover window further buffers against cold‑start risk.

03 · £0.69M / YR

Mid‑shift tech fault

Today: When a tech fault hits, some events are cancelled but continuity is the priority — events are cancelled as‑we‑go and whenever the studio is back operational, work resumes. Now: With 6 studios firing staggered, a single studio going down loses at worst its remaining matches. The other 5 keep the feed alive; broadcast continuity is maintained.

04 · £0.59M / YR

Gamer fatigue errors

Today: 1‑on‑1‑off cadence produces late‑session mistakes — each match is 4 × 5‑min quarters (+ up to 4 × 3‑min OT) inside a 32‑min slot, and alternating all shift wears gamers down. Disputed results climb, void rate rises. Now: Fatigue‑safe rotation caps play at 3 in a row (max twice/shift), with minimum 2‑match blocks and guaranteed rest gaps. Cleaner settlements, fewer voids.

05 · £0.33M / YR

Slot‑boundary void

Today: When a shift ends abruptly, in‑flight markets get voided. Now: 32‑min slots line up cleanly with the shift window. S4–6 last match is cancelled by design (shift overlap), so there's no rush to finish — markets close in natural match order, not by clock.

06 · CONTINUOUS CAREER PROGRESSION

Track the whole career. Focus on the last 8 weeks.

01

No seasonal resets — performance accumulates.

A gamer's full career history stays on record. Lifetime stats, total shifts completed, historical tier positions — all tracked continuously. There are no artificial "season starts" that wipe the slate. A gamer who's proven themselves over 200 shifts has a richer profile than one with 20.

02

8‑week rolling SPS average drives tier position.

The primary ranking metric is the rolling average of the last 8 weeks of SPS scores. This window is long enough to smooth out bad days, short enough to reward sustained improvement. A 4/wk committed gamer refreshes their window in 2 weeks; a 1/wk light gamer takes 8 weeks for a full 8‑shift sample. Both are on the same scale.

03

Peer overlap is structural, not accidental.

Every Night shift you work, you're on the same 24‑gamer roster across 6 studios (Day: 16 gamers, 4 studios). With the fatigue‑safe rotation, every gamer faces every other gamer in their studio across the shift. The head‑to‑head sample is real and feeds cleanly into the rolling SPS alongside the density/narrative/reliability components.

04

"Career in the Studio" unlocks reliability multipliers.

Longevity and consistency compound into real pay. Academy (entry, 0%) → Rookie (50+ shifts, <6% cancel, +2.5%) → Regular (promoted from D3, <4%, +7.5%) → Specialist (1+ month top 3, <3%, +12.5%) → Elite (3+ months D1, <2%, +30%) → Champion (division title holder, 1 per division, 8+ wins/shift, +35%). Each step boosts the reliability multiplier on every shift, for the rest of the career.

05

Broadcast gets a continuous narrative arc.

No "pre‑season" dead air. With a new match closing every ~10 minutes and 24 gamers ranked live across 6 studios, the bug/lower‑third has fresh content continuously. "Torres breaks into Elite after 6 months" is a storyline that builds over weeks, not a reset artefact.

06

Day vs Night: parallel ladders, shared career.

Each shift pool runs its own tier ladder. But career stats, reliability tier, and bonus multiplier are portable — a gamer's "Career in the Studio" profile follows them. End‑of‑month "Championship Night" (Day T1 vs Night T1) becomes the marquee cross‑pool event.

This restructure is built for where SIS is going, not just where it is.

SIS is scaling its Competitive Gaming portfolio aggressively. SIS now headlines 275,000+ live events a year across Competitive Gaming, while earlier Hard Rock Bet coverage specified 225,000+ annual eBasketball and eSoccer games and a launch into the 15th US state. Every metric that matters to partners — content volume, feed reliability, match integrity — depends on the same operational foundation this restructure builds: lower cancel rates, measurable gamer quality, fatigue‑safe scheduling, and 24/7 continuity.

The restructure delivers the infrastructure for scale: Night 129 + Day 86 matches/shift (target: 129 + 129 post Day S5–6 expansion) at a target 1% cancel rate, SPS‑driven career tracking that makes gamer quality auditable, and a title‑agnostic structure that EAFC and eAmerican Football can inherit after NBA proves out. The Night validation window on Studios 5–6 validates the model at zero incremental cost before scaling.
