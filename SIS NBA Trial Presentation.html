CONTEXT № 02 / 11
SIS THE PROBLEM

Cancellations happen in three ways — and they happen often.

01 · LATE OR ABSENT
The no-show void
If a player isn't present within roughly ten minutes of the scheduled time, the fixture is voided.
02 · TECH FAULTS
Faults & crashes
Equipment issues and random game crashes — before, during, or after a match starts — that aren't resolved in time.
03 · OTHER OPS ISSUES
Other floor issues
Other technical or people-management issues that stop a scheduled fixture from running.
HOW OFTEN, HONESTLY
No-shows happen very often — sometimes several times in a single day. When one does, the schedule is amended: the missing player is dropped and a 3-man operation rotates in their place, unless a suitable replacement is found in time. These 3-man shifts are one of the most common complaints on the floor.

SIS DIAGNOSIS

None of this is a mystery to anyone who has worked a shift.

01
Repetitive, no-stakes cadence
Every match is the same match. Nothing about the format changes when a shift is going well or badly.
02
No progression
A good week and a bad week look identical. There is no visible "up" to play for.
03
Flat, attendance-based pay
Pay tracks hours in the chair, not reliability or performance.
04
A bonus system nobody trusts
The current bonus structure and the ELO-style ranking are openly ridiculed by gamers — they carry no credibility on the floor.
05
Cancelling is the only pressure valve
People push to cancel strategically to gain break time. That is a format failure, not something to pin on individuals.
06
Sick, not absent
People also come in sick rather than lose pay or standing — which puts the people around them at risk too. Expected to ease as the operation stabilises.
07
Nothing has been tried, properly
None of the above has ever been tested against a real alternative — until now.

THE OPPORTUNITY № 05 / 11
SIS THE PROPOSAL

Three changes. Same pay, same staffing.

CONCEPT / 01
Format
Studios 5–6 run as divisions with promotion and relegation. Stakes on every shift, not just the marquee ones.
CONCEPT / 02
Schedule
A 10-minute stagger paired with a fatigue-safe rotation — no gamer carries more than three matches in a row without guaranteed rest.
CONCEPT / 03
Progression
An SPS-based visible rank covering skill, reliability, and attendance — not attendance alone.
ALL OF IT AT CURRENT PAY AND STAFFING · £0 INCREMENTAL PAYROLL
THE PROPOSAL № 06 / 11
SIS HOW A SHIFT RUNS

A loop, repeated every shift.

STEP 01
Seed
Studios 5–6 are placed into standings order from the prior shift's results.
STEP 02
Play
The rotation runs — staggered starts, fatigue-safe pairings, every match on the table.
STEP 03
Table
Live standings update after every match — visible on the floor as the shift plays out.
STEP 04
Promote / Relegate
Top of the table moves up, bottom moves down. Stakes reset for the next shift.
STEP 05
Reseed
The next shift starts on new standings — new stakes, same rotation.
↻ REPEATS · EVERY SHIFT, EVERY NIGHT
Same five steps across all 42 trial nights — the point is a predictable loop gamers can actually plan around, shift to shift.
HOW A SHIFT RUNS № 07 / 11
SIS THE 10-MINUTE STAGGER

A ten-minute head start, six times over.

Staggered starts give every studio its own pre-show buffer to absorb a delay or a late change.
A single studio's tech fault no longer risks the whole feed's rhythm.
Once all six studios are live, a match closes roughly every ten minutes — steady for broadcast and betting continuity.
